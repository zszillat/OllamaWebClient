write me a short story about bees
The dawn sun drifted through the orchard’s apple blossoms, warming the dew-speckled petals and stirring a gentle hum in the air. In the heart of an old hollow oak, a young worker bee named Lila unfurled her wings for the very first forage. Her sisters—veteran foragers with stripes dulled by years of sun and pollen—greeted her with patient buzzes and guiding dances. Today, Lila would learn how to read the language of flowers. Flying low among the blossoms, Lila felt the soft tickle of pollen against her legs. She hovered beside a blossom heavy with nectar, dipping her tongue in and savoring the sweet reward of her labor. Nearby, a bee elder performed the “waggle dance,” wiggling her body in a figure-eight to show the best patch of golden daffodils just beyond the creek. Lila watched, memorizing the angle of the dance and the vigor of each wiggle. Trusting the colony’s teachings, she set off in a smooth, purposeful arc across the orchard floor. By mid-afternoon, Lila returned laden with bright yellow pollen sacs, her wings humming with quiet pride. Inside the hive, the colony greeted her with welcoming buzzes as she shared her harvest. The young bee elder, once nervous about her first flight, now basked in the warm gratitude of her hive sisters. Together, they built honeycomb walls thicker than before, ensuring the brood’s safety and the spring’s bounty. Outside, blossoms swayed and the orchard thrummed with life—proof that even the smallest bee, guided by kindness and community, could help the entire world blossom.
